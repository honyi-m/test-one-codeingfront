  • Turkiye

    -I have traveled to Turkey and it was a truly unforgettable experience. This beautiful country is full of amazing places to visit, from incredible historical monuments to breathtaking natural landscapes. The food is truly amazing – every meal was a feast of flavors and I enjoyed every bite. I truly love this country and it holds a special place in my heart. On this trip, I also went to Istanbul, a city that is alive and well even at night, and I love its energetic atmosphere and lively nightlife. Traveling to Turkey was not just a trip, but a deep connection with a culture that I admire so much. Now I want to introduce you to some of the country's popular sights and foods.

    • Places to visit:
      • Boğaz Köprüsü

        The Boğaz Köprüsü or Bosphorus Bridge in Istanbul is the first suspension bridge connecting the continents of Asia and Europe and was opened in 1973. The bridge is mainly for vehicles and pedestrians are only allowed to cross once a year, as free walking on the bridge has been restricted due to numerous suicides.

      • Cappadocia

        Cappadocia is a unique and spectacular region in Turkey, known for its amazing rock landscapes, rock-cut houses, and famous hot air balloon rides. Here, history and nature blend together and it is a popular destination for tourists from all over the world.

      • Hagia Sophia

        Hagia Sophia is a magnificent historical building in Istanbul, first a church, then a mosque, now a museum, and then a mosque again. This unique architectural masterpiece symbolizes the fusion of Christian and Islamic cultures and has been of special importance since the 6th century AD to the present day.

    • Foods:
      • Doner Kebab

        Doner Kebab is one of the most popular Turkish dishes, made from chicken, lamb or beef and grilled vertically on a rotating skewer. The grilled meat is sliced thinly and is usually served inside pita bread or with rice and vegetables. This dish is also popular in many countries and is one of the symbols of Turkish street food.


        Recipe Doner Kebab
      • Lahmacun

        Lahmacun is a popular Turkish and Middle Eastern dish that is similar to pizza, but thinner and softer. A mixture of minced meat (usually beef or lamb), onions, tomatoes, vegetables, and special spices such as red pepper and paprika are spread on a thin dough and then baked in the oven. It is usually served with lemon and fresh herbs and is a light and delicious dish.


        Recipe Lahmacun
      • Baklava

        Baklava is a traditional and very popular Turkish and Middle Eastern dessert. It is made from thin layers of filo pastry, between which are placed chopped walnuts or pistachios, and after baking, it is soaked in a sweet syrup (honey or sugar syrup). With its sweet taste and soft and crispy texture, baklava is one of the most delicious desserts in the region.


        Recipe Baklava
      • Simit

        Simit is a traditional and popular Turkish bread, shaped like a ring and topped with sesame seeds. It is usually eaten as a breakfast or snack, accompanied by Turkish tea or cheese and olives. Crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, simit is one of the simple and delicious symbols of the streets of Istanbul.

  • Tabriz

    I am a Turk myself and I am always proud of my city. Tabriz is a historical and cultural city in the northwest of Iran that is full of interesting and fascinating places. From the Grand Bazaar of Tabriz, which is one of the oldest covered markets in the world, to the Blue Mosque with its unique tilework, every corner of the city is full of stories and history. The cuisine of Tabriz is both famous and well-known; Tabrizi meatballs, dolma, doogh soup and sweets such as baghlama, each of which has a special and hearty taste and turns every meal into a celebration. The Azerbaijani Turkish language spoken in this city is an inseparable part of the people's identity and has shaped the rich culture, local music and customs of the Tabrizis. Tabriz is a vibrant city that both holds history in its heart and experiences modern life. The kind and hospitable people of this city make a trip to Tabriz memorable for every tourist. I always like to introduce the culture and beauty of Tabriz to others and I hope that one day you will see this beautiful city and fall in love with it. Now I want to introduce you to some of the popular sights and foods of this country.

    • Places to visit:
      • Tabriz Grand Bazaar

        Tabriz Grand Bazaa is one of the oldest and largest covered markets in the world, dating back several hundred years. In addition to being the center of trade and economy of the region, this market is also considered a place for the exchange of culture and art. With beautiful traditional architecture, it has various sections for selling various goods such as carpets, spices, handicrafts and clothing, and is one of the main tourist attractions of Tabriz.

      • Shah Goli Palace (El Goli Park)

        Shah Goli Palace, located within El Goli Park in Tabriz, is a beautiful historic mansion set on an artificial lake. Built during the Qajar era, it served as a royal summer residence. The palace is surrounded by lush gardens and offers a peaceful retreat with stunning views of the water and the city. El Goli Park itself is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike to relax, enjoy nature, and experience traditional tea houses nearby.

      • Saat Tower (Municipality Building)

        Saat Tower, also known as the Tabriz Municipality Building, is a historic and architectural symbol of the city. It was built in the early 20th century and is famous for its large, prominent clock located at the top of the building. The tower features a unique architectural style and is situated in the city center near the Tabriz Grand Bazaar. Today, besides its administrative function, it serves as an important tourist attraction for visitors

      • Tabriz Citadel (Arg of Tabriz)

        Tabriz Citadel, also known as Arg of Tabriz, is an important historical structure located in the heart of Tabriz. It dates back to ancient times and once served as a center of governance and defense for the city. The remaining ruins of the citadel still attract many tourists due to their historical significance.

    • Foods:
      • Tabrizi Meatball

        Tabrizi meatballs are a traditional and popular dish from the city of Tabriz, consisting of large balls of minced meat, rice or bulgur, vegetables, and various spices. The meatballs are usually filled with walnuts, eggs, or prunes, and are served with tomato sauce after cooking.


        Recipe Tabrizi Meatball
      • Dolma

        Dolma is a popular traditional dish in Iran, especially in Tabriz, consisting of rolled vegetable leaves or hair leaves stuffed with a mixture of rice, minced meat, aromatic herbs, and spices. Dolma is usually stewed or baked in the oven and has a fragrant and delicious flavor.


        Recipe Dolma
      • Qarabiyyeh

        Qarabiyyeh is a traditional and popular Tabriz sweet, usually made with almonds or coconut powder. This soft and delicious sweet, with a mild flavor and delicate texture, is one of the most popular desserts at Tabriz celebrations and parties, and is a reminder of the rich confectionery culture of the region.


        Recipe Qarabiyyeh
  • Isfahan

    I have traveled to Isfahan and it was truly an unforgettable experience. This historic city with its magnificent architecture and beautiful bridges such as Si-Vose-Pol and Khaju-Pol, fascinates every visitor. Naqsh-e-Jahan Square with its surrounding mosques and palaces such as Imam Mosque and Ali-Qapu is a symbol of Iranian culture and art, where history can be felt in every corner. The food of Isfahan is also very delicious and diverse; dishes such as Isfahan biryani, Sultani kebab and local stews, each of which has a special and pleasant taste and makes the trip more enjoyable. The culture and art of this city are also clearly visible in the music, handicrafts such as khatam kari and carpet weaving. Isfahan is a city that beautifully blends history and modern life, and its kind and hospitable people made my trip to this city a memorable one. I hope everyone can visit this beautiful city one day and enjoy its beauty. Now I want to introduce you to some of the popular sights and foods of this country.

    • Places to visit:
      • Naqsh-e Jahan Square

        Naqsh-e Jahan Square in Isfahan, built during the Safavid era, is one of the largest historical squares in the world. It is surrounded by the Shah Mosque, Sheikh Lotfollah Mosque, Ali Qapu Mansion, and Qaysariyeh Bazaar, and is listed on the UNESCO World Heritage List.

      • Si-o-se-pol Bridge

        Si-Woseh-Pol of Isfahan is a historic bridge from the Safavid era over the Zayandeh-Rud River, built with 33 spans and functioning as a bridge and dam at the same time; this bridge has also been a venue for traditional celebrations and ceremonies.

      • Chehel Sotoun Palace

        Chehelsotun Palace in Isfahan is a garden-like building from the Safavid period, which, with its twenty wooden columns on its porch and their reflection in the pond in front, gives the impression of "forty columns." It was built for official receptions and celebrations of Shah Abbas II.

    • Foods:
      • Isfahan Beryan

        Briyon of Isfahan is a traditional and delicious dish made from cooked mutton and special spices. This hot and popular dish is usually served with fresh bread and has a unique flavor.
